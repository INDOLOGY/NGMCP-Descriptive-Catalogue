Manuscript Features

A numbered folio (the figure 134 is written in the middle right-hand margin on the verso), which, however, is not related to the text and was microfilmed thrice, appears between fols. 67 and 68.

The MS is written in a different hand from fol. 73v onwards.

Excerpts

Beginning

❖ oṃ namaś caṇḍikāyai ||

śrīgaṇeśā[ya] namaḥ ||

yad yogibhir bhavabhayārttivināśayogya,m

āsādya vanditam atīva viviktacittaiḥ |

tad vaḥ punātu haripādasarojayugma,m

āvirbhavat kramavilaṃghitabhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ ||

sā pātu vaḥ sakalayogijanasya citte,

[ʼ]vidyātamiśrataraṇir bhuvi muktihetuḥ |

yā cānyajantunivahasya vimohanīti-,

māyāvidher jjagati śuddhasu(!)buddhihantrī ||

īśvaraṃ jagatām ādyaṃ praṇamya puruṣottamaṃ |

nityajñānamayaṃ vakṣye, purāṇaṃ kālikāhvayaṃ ||

mārkkaṇḍeyaṃ muniśreṣṭhaṃ, sthitaṃ himadharāntike |

munayaḥ paripapracchuḥ praṇamya kamaṭhādayaḥ ||

bhagavan samyag ākhyātaṃ, sarvvaśāstrāṇi tattvataḥ |

vedān sarvvān(!)s tathā śā(!)ṅgān, sārabhūtaṃ pramathya ca || (fol. 1v1–5)

End

yo lokam īśaḥ satataṃ bibhartti

yaḥ pālayed antakaraś ca yaḥ syāt |

idaṃ samasta(!) trayam atra śaśvat

tadīyarūpaṃ ca namo [ʼ]stu tasmai ||

pradhānapuruṣau yasya prapañcau yogināṃ hṛdi |

yaḥ purāṇādhipo viṣṇuḥ prasīdatu sanātanaḥ ||

yo hetur ugraḥ puruṣaḥ purāṇaḥ

purāṇakṛd vedapurāṇakebhyaḥ ||

paraḥ parebhyaś ca purārirūpo

dhyeyaṃ ca tannāma purāṇaśeṣe ||

iti sakalajagad bibhartti yā sā

madhuripumohakarī namo [ʼ]stu tasyai |

vahati ca vapuṣā maheśvaro yāṃ

praṇamata prāṇihitāṃ śivān tāṃ ||     || (fol. 272v10–273r3)

Colophon

iti śrīkālikāpurāṇaṃ samāptaṃ || 88 || 89 || ❁ ||

kāle dyocāraṇavikalite, śuddhanepālaśāke,

māghe māse śaśadharadine, kālikākhyaṃ purāṇaṃ |

śrīpañcamyāṃ dharaṇi,vibudhaś cakrarājaḥ sudhīraḥ

śukle pakṣe vividharacanaṃ lekhayāmāsa kena ||

«According to Kāśināth, kāle dyocāraṇavikalite is a paraphrase of the date 906 given below; he takes kāla- to represent “9” (cf. Apte s.v. kāla 6, “Time considered as one of the nine dravyas by the Vaiśeṣikas), dyo° to represent “0”, and cāraṇa- to represent “6” (with cāraṇa- being an abbreviation for ṣaṭcāraṇa- = ṣaṭcaraṇa- “having six legs, bee”); and Nepāla Śāka would be the same as Nepāla Saṃvat.

But I am not sure, if it is that easy; what about °vikalite (indicating that something is to be subtracted), and should the figure-paraphrasing words in the compound not be given in reverse order?»

saṃ 906 saṃpūrṇṇaṃ || (fol. 273r3–5)
