Manuscript Features

1. The MS contains two texts:

  • (1) Saṃkṣiptakāśīvijayanāṭaka (exp.11t–18, 2–4t)
  • (2) Ugracaṇḍādevīnityarcanavidhi (exp. 4b–10)

2. Exps. 2 and 18, and 5 and 6 are two exposures of the same folio.

3. Much of the dialogue and the Newari stage directions are left out. The result is a shortened and incomplete version of the Kāśīvijayanāṭaka (A 345/10), as the following comparison shows:

4. The king Nareśa Malla mentioned here reigned in Bhaktapur from NS 757 āśvina to 763 āśvina. But here he is mentioned in NS 753 bhādra, a date added later in the left margin of the text. Dvitīya sureśa in Mepu no. 4 is in reference to him.
