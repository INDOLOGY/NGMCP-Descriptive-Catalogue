Foliation There are two foliations, one in the left- and one in the right-hand margin. The latter is continuous from beginning to end, with some misnumbering and lack of foliation. (Sometimes there are two folio numbers on the right: one the continuous number, and the other the folio number within the current paṭala.) The left-hand foliation is more erratic: sometimes it recommences at the beginning of a paṭala, and sometimes runs on, and sometimes the foliation is out of order. In any case, the text as it appears on the microfilm is in sequential order. The foliation cited throughout this catalogue entry refers to the number in the right-hand margin. Folios with no folio number are referred to by exposure number.

Fols. 1–24: figures in the upper left-hand margin under the abbreviation meru. and in the lower right-hand margin under the word taṃtra on the verso.

Fols. 25–48: figures in the extreme lower right-hand margin on the verso.

Fols. 49–63: figures in the upper left-hand margin and in the extreme lower right-hand margin on the verso.

From fol. 64 (right margin) on, the folio numbers in the two margins vary: the foliation in the upper left-hand margin begins with 71. Sometimes the figure in the right-hand margin appears in the middle rather than in the lower part of the margin. In some cases, the figures in either margin are illegible, and in other cases the figures have been corrected.
