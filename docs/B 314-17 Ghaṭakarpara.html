Manuscript Features

The text as given in the present MS consists of 21 stanzas, whereas several editions of the text consist of 22 stanzas. Stanza 21 of these editions (beginning with etan niśamya) is missing in the present MS.

As mentioned by the scribe in the stanzas following the colophon, the Ghaṭakarpara is particularly distinguished by the use of yamakas (ghaṭakarpakṛtakāvyaṃ ślokaṃ yamaka­cihnakaṃ).

Excerpts

Beginning

❖ oṃ gaṇapataye namaḥ ||

nicitaṃ kham upetya nīradaiḥ

priyahīnā hṛdayāvanīradaiḥ

salilair nihitaṃ rajaḥ kṣitau

ravicaṃdrāv api nopalakṣitau ||

haṃsā nadanmeghabhayād dravanti

niśāmukhāny adya na candravanti |

na cāmbumattāḥ śikhino nadanti

meghāgame kundasamānadanti || (fol. 1v1–3)

End

bhāvānuraktavanitāsurataiḥ śapeyam

ālabhya cāmbutṛṣitaḥ karakośapeyaṃ |

jīyeya yena kavi[[nā]] yamakaiḥ pareṇa

tasmai vaheyam udakaṃ ghaṭakarppareṇa || ○ || (fol. 3r6–3v2)

Colophon

iti ghaṭakarparakāvyaṃ samāptaṃ || ||

❖ netrarandhrahaye varṣe kārttike kṛṣṇapakṣake |

caturthyāṃ bhṛguje vāre punarvasūttamo ʼtrake ||

ghaṭakarpakṛtakāvyaṃ ślokaṃ yamakacihnakaṃ |

lilekha pramanā (!) yatnāt śivapūrvvaharir dvijaḥ || (fol. 3v3)
