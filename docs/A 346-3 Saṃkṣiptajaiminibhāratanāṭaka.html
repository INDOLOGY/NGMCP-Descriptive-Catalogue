Subject Nāṭaka

Language Sanskrit and Newari

Text Features The author of this unpublished melodious drama written in corrupt Sanskrit is Jitāmitra Malla, which is indicated by the word ‘bhaṇita’ in the Prastāvanā Aṅka and in several songs. The same is indicated in the colophon: jitāmitramalladevena nāṭakaṃ siddhaṃ. This nāṭaka is said to be staged by (dayakā juro) the Crown Prince Bhupatīndra Malla , which meant here he managed the nāṭaka to be staged.

This text has been written in the time of the joint rule of Jitāmitra Malla and his brother Ugra Malla along with the Crown Prince Bhūpatīndra Malla. Jitāmitra Malla and Ugra Malla are seen as the joint rulers in NS 801 / AD 1681 in Viṣṇumaṇḍala paubhā (Slusser, vol. II, plate 383).

Two versions of this nāṭaka, abridged (A 346/3) and complete (A 346/8). The dates given in both versions are the same

NS 809-ghaṃtījyā ārambha (text being composed)

NS 809- ghaṃtījyā dhuna / dhunakā (text completed)

NS 810 pauṣaśudi 3 - ṛ tayā (music being composed) in 346/8; - ārambha yāṅā dina (opening day) in A 346/3
