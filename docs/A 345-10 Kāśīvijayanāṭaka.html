Manuscript Features:

1. Fols. 4–9 are missing.

2. There are two exposures of fols. 44v–45r

3. The authorship of Rāmacandra

Dhakal (1995 5) mentions in his edition of the Harikeli, an epic by Vaṃśamaṇi, that no original work by the poet's father, Rāmacandra, has ever been found. Here we have a nāṭaka by him. Though the author does not mention his name in Prastāvanā aṅka (initial introductory act) from mepu (song number) 21 to the last (120), he mentions both his name and that of the king Tribhuvana Malla. Before his name occur the verbs gāe (‘sung,’ song no. 21) bola (‘spoken’ no. 35), kahaya (‘said’ no. 36), bhāne (‘told’ no. 42), gāyalo (‘sang’ no.120) etc. This certainly proves that the author of this nāṭaka is Vipra Rāmacandra. He is said to be the father of Vaṃśamaṇi kavi of the Bhāradvāja gotra and to have hailed from Janakajanapada (modern Janakpur in southern Nepal) in the Sanskrit nāṭaka of Vaṃśamaṇi's Gītadigambara, which was staged in the time of the king Pratap Malla of Kathmandu.
