Author Vīracandra

Subject Tantra

Language Sanskrit

Text Features Manual on the daily rites in śaiva tantric religious practice. The author is called śaivācaryaśrīvīracandra in one sub-colophon, another work of his is mentioned, the Caturviṃśatiprakāśa. Other works are quoted, as Ciñciṇīnātha’s Kramanirṇaya. This school seems to belong to the western branch of śaiva tantras (paścimānvaya). In the Nityakaumudī, a commentary on this text by Gaṅgādhara, it is said that the Nityaprakāśa was compiled in the year 994 (cf. B 34/11).

Manuscript Features

The following fols. are extant: 11–14; 16; 19. The writing on the verso of fol. 19 is partly rubbed off. In the left-hand margin of fol. 16v has been written the following in Devanagari characters: nityaprakāśa vīracandrasya.
