Manuscript Features

In 1970 Tarapada Mukharji published an edition of the Gopīcandranāṭaka with an introduction, notes and glossary from Calcutta University. It is based on the Cambridge manuscript of a 17th century Bengali play from Nepal.

Cecil Bendall in his Catalogue of the Buddhist Sanskrit Manuscripts in the University Library, Cambridge

(Stuttgart : Franz Steiner Verlag, 1992. Reprint) notes regarding add. 1389: Gopī–candra–nāṭaka ( vernacular )

  1. Siddhinarasiṃha is mentioned at 61a.
  2. Govinda-candra , is mentioned as “Bangero adhipati” at 1.b , 1.1 .

Mukharji’s Gopīcandranāṭaka seems to be the same as the one in Bendall’s Catalogue. The following Bengali-Newari Gopīcandranāṭakas given below seems to be related to the published nāṭaka.

Inv. No. / Microfilmed No. / Special remarks

39488 A 345/12 Siddhinarasiṃha is mentioned (fol.53).

39489 A 345/13 Śrīnivāsa Malla and "Bangero adhipati" Govinda-candra are mentioned (fol.54).

39490 A 345/15 Guru Jālandhara and King Gopīcandra are mentioned (fol.12).

39492 A 346/5 Siddhinarasiṃha Malla and "Baṅgadeśera adhipati" Gopīcandra are mentioned (fol.53).
